A11.10 Language focus: Cause and effect
Conjunctions are words that link the different parts of a sentence to express cause and effect. The
most important conjunctions are: because, as, since, and so.
Because, as, and since introduce a cause; so introduces an effect. They are used to join two complete
sentences (or independent clauses) together. They are often used like this:
First sentence Conjunction Second sentence
I stayed at home
It was raining,
because
so
it was raining.
I stayed at home.
You can also reverse the order of the sentences with because, as, and since, for example:
Because it was raining, I stayed at home.
Note that this is not possible with so.
