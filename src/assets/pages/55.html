B3.2 Fun with words: Play a game
Work in groups to list each of the hobbies mentioned or collected in Part A in the order
of their importance, popularity, cost and so on. Create a pack of statement cards for each
hobby (for example, photography is an expensive hobby).
How to play:
Each person in the group picks up a card and reads out the statement. Players take turns to say
●
if they agree or disagree and the reasons for their opinion.
Try to continue each discussion for at least five minutes. Use the expressions that you have
●
learned for agreeing, disagreeing, asking for opinions, interrupting and so on.
B3.3 Reading: The karate lesson
This extract comes from a book called Nosipho and the King of Bones by Ginny Swart. Set in
Cape Town, South Africa, the story is about Nosipho, a secondary school student who lives
with her mother and her young brother, Mondli. Nosipho’s classmate, Phindile, has come to
the house and is giving Mondli his first lesson in karate.
Nosipho sat on the back step and watched as Phindile instructed her brother.
“Now in karate, a man needs no weapons except his own body,” he began.
“Ya! Kick him! I want to learn how to kick people!” shouted Mondli.
“Oh no, my friend, that is not the way of a karataka,” smiled Phindile.
“A what?”
“That’s what a student of karate is called,” he explained. “A karataka is a man of peace. But he is so
well trained and has so much power in his body that whenever he needs it, he can explode with strength
and can overcome anybody, even someone three times his own size.”
“Explode with strength! I want to be a … what you said,” breathed Mondli, his eyes shining.
“Well, I can show you how to begin. But after that you should have proper instruction from the Dan
at the Youth Club,” said Phindile. “All karatakas must learn thirty different fighting exercises. They are
called kata. Like this!”
