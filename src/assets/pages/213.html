B11.11 Language focus: Relative clauses
Complete the following sentences with the relative pronoun who, whom, which or that.
1 This is the bank _______ was robbed yesterday.
2 A boy _______ sister is in my class was in the bank at the time.
3 She told me _______ the man _______ robbed the bank had a gun.
4 He wore a mask _______ covered his face and hair.
5 The robber had come with a friend _______ waited in a car outside the bank.
6 The customers _______ were in the bank were very frightened.
7 One of the cashiers pressed an alarm _______ was connected to the police station.
8 The robber in the bank grabbed the money _______ the cashier handed over to him.
9 He ran towards the door _______ led outside into the street.
10 He jumped into the car _______ was waiting outside the bank but he didn’t know the police
had already arrested his friend and were waiting for him!
We use relative clauses to give additional information about something without starting another
sentence. By combining sentences with a relative clause using relative pronouns such as who, whom,
which or that, you can avoid repeating certain words.
